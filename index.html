Explanation of floating in water

The force felt by an object immersed in a liquid is equal to the weight of the liquid removed by the object. So a ship of two thousand tons will continue to sink until it removes two thousand tons of water. If the ship removes two thousand tons of water before it sinks, then the ship will float and not sink. This will be possible if the density of the vessel is less than the density of water removed by the vessel. This is done by leaving space inside the ship. As a result, the ship floats after removing the water of equal weight.
